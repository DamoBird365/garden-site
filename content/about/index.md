## 👋 Hello, I'm Damien!

I'm a keen gardener based in **Aberdeen, Northeast Scotland**. This site is my garden journal — a place to document what I'm growing, share tips for gardening in the Scottish climate, and hopefully inspire others to give it a go.

### 🌍 About This Site

Living in NE Scotland means dealing with:
- Late frosts (often into mid-May!)
- Short growing seasons
- Plenty of wind and rain
- But also long summer days and surprisingly mild coastal temperatures

This site features:
- **Blog posts** documenting my garden progress with photos
- **Planting calendar** tailored for Northeast Scotland
- **Weather updates** from Aberdeen alongside each post
- **Seasonal guides** for what to plant and when

### 🥕 What I Grow

I focus on vegetables that thrive (or at least survive!) in the Scottish climate: carrots, parsnips, potatoes, onions, peas, broad beans, kale, lettuce, beetroot, broccoli, leeks, turnips, and — with the help of a greenhouse — tomatoes, peppers, courgettes, and sweetcorn.
